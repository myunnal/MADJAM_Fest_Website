GAME

The MAD Game Jam competition challenges several teams to create, in 48 hours in a row, video games on a theme that they will only meet on the first day of the event. This competition takes place last weekend in February and promotes a comprehensive and creative look at game development. A space of intensive creation that seeks in the intersections between areas of knowledge new creative approaches to the universe of computer games.

Rules:

  • The teams develop the games in 48 hours, on a theme that they will only know on the first day.
  • The papers are presented to a panel of juries after about 48 hours.
  • Candidates can apply alone or in teams of up to 6 members, presenting the areas they want to explore.

The presentation of the works after 48 hours will be designed and subject to evaluation, where the 3 best classifieds will be awarded in addition to being awarded 4 honorable mentions for non-awarded teams.

VIDEO

The MAD Video Jam competition, challenges several teams to create in 48 hours in a row, audiovisual projects on a theme that will only be revealed at the beginning of the event. This competition is held last weekend in February and promotes a comprehensive and creative look at the development of audiovisual projects, namely: short films, documentaries, advertising spots, experimental videos, music videos, among others. MAD Video Jam is a space of intensive creation that seeks intersections between areas of knowledge and new creative approaches in the audiovisual universe.

Rules:

  • The teams develop the projects in 48 hours, on a theme that they will only know on the first day.
  • The papers are presented to a panel of juries after about 48 hours.
  • Candidates can apply alone or in teams of up to 8 members, presenting the areas they want to explore.
  • All projects must be filmed within the predefined area on the map.
  • All projects must have a duration of between 1 to 5 minutes.
  • All projects must include at least one scene filmed at ESMAD.
  • All projects must include at least one interior scene and one exterior scene.
  • At midnight each day, it is introducing a small twist, a new sub-theme that complicates the whole competition a little more, in this case: filming a specific location on the map + including a specific object in the project.
  • Participants must be autonomous in the equipment necessary for the development of the contents.
  • All participants are entitled to free lunches and dinners.

The presentation of the works after 48 hours will be designed and subject to evaluation, where the 3 best classifieds will be awarded in addition to being awarded 4 honorable mentions for non-awarded teams.

PHOTO

The MAD Photo Jam challenges participants to develop an original photographic project in intensive format and over 48 hours, on a theme that they will only know on the first day of the event. Participants must be autonomous in the equipment necessary for the development of the Project. The works will be presented at the end of the 48 hours at the event and in exhibition format after the event.

Rules:

  • There is only one category, regardless of the technique(s) used.
  • Each Project must include 10 images.
  • The participants develop the work on a theme that they will only know on the first day.
  • The works are presented by the author and projected to the public 48 hours later.
  • Participants must be autonomous in the equipment necessary for the development of the contents.

The presentation of the works after 48 hours will be designed and subject to evaluation, where the 3 best classifieds will be awarded in addition to being awarded 4 honorable mentions for non-awarded teams+.

ANIMATION

Open Call for intensive artistic creations in motion design/animation, crossing diverse techniques to respond to a theme that participants will only know on the first day of the event. These techniques can involve both the creation of 2D and 3D motion/animation, and where participants have the opportunity to explore exclusively digital content in 48 hours. This will be a space for experimentation, artistic creation, reflection and immersion. The works can be produced in linear, interactive or contemplative animation format.

Rules:

  • The teams develop the work in 48 hours on a theme that they will only know on the same day.
  • The works will be presented to the public after 48 hours.
  • Applicants may apply individually or groups of up to 5 participants.
  • All projects must have a minimum duration of 20 seconds and a maximum of 2 minutes, using exclusively digital content. No AI platform can be used to create content/assets. However, generative content can be created (Processing, Touch Designer.).
  • After the dinner of the 1st day will be introduced a small twist, a new subtheme that will create a new challenge for what remains of the competition (location/object/theme/etc.)
  • Participants must be autonomous in the equipment necessary for the development of the contents.
  • All participants are entitled to free lunches and dinners for the duration of Motion Jam.
  • At the end of the 48 hours, they must submit the project developed for the presentation and evaluation of the jury (to be communicated in due course).
  • Participants must grant the right to images of the projects developed in the context of the dissemination of the event.

After the screening, the jury will meet and announce the winner.
